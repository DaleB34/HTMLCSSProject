The "Science" of Sock Loss

Many more, inferior, researchers before me have come up with a more "scientific," "measurable," and "logical" reason that the number of socks you put in your laundry are lower than when you take it out.

From the scientists at Samsung , a formula has been found, linking the phenomonon of losing socks to a simple math equation, The Sock Loss Index represented by the equation (L+C)-(P x A).

Variable Variable Meaning
Laundry Size (L) Number of people in household * frequency of washes in a week
Washing Complexity (C) Types of washes (darks + whites) * number of socks washed in a week
Positivity towards doing laundry Measured on scale from 1 to 5, 1 being very negative, 5 being very positive
Degree of Attention (A) Sum of number of activities being done at the same time
